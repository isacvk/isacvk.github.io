A LITTLE BIT ABOUT ME

Welcome to story time with Isac!

Background

I was born in the little village of Karnataka, Inida. At the time, the village had no electricity supply. I was in primary school when we got an electricity connection, and my dad bought a laptop when I was in high school. It was a Lenovo B560 and all I did in that laptop was play Need For Speed. I didn't know what else to do because no one in my family knew how to use a laptop, nor did we have an Internet connection.

How it all started

When I finished my high school, my neighbour, also my uncle bought a WiFi. And that's when the creative journey began. I decided to learn photoshop during this time of vacation. I go to his house, download a bunch of photoshop tutorials, go back home and practice. My entire vacation went like this and within a year I learned nearly all the important tools and techniques of photoshop.

Imposter syndrome

I was able to redesign just about any design, but when it came to designing my own things, I didn't know what to do. I thought I wasn't creative and that's why I can't develop my own designs. And it took me 3 years to figure out that creativity is something I can develop as I practise more and more.

College. Why?

By the time I realized this I was in college doing my BCA graduation. Time flies ha ha. This was such an awesome and wonderful period in my life, I hated going to college. Would you like to know why? Because I paid so much less to Udemy to learn much more than I ever learned at college. Udemy was my go place to learn new skills, along with YouTube, obviously.

The passion towards art

This was the time I started learning music production, I started with video editing, I fell in love with good storytelling and mainly UI/UX. As I was familiar with Photoshop, it was quite easy for me to learn Figma. I would say that it just came naturally to me, I was designing things in Figma in no time. I really got interested in the field and started learning about it on YouTube. I buy courses on Udemy only if I feel like I want to keep learning in this area.

First client

At that time I had an opportunity to work with a client and I didn't want to miss that opportunity. It was a full stack web application and I was responsible of the UI and back-end. I wasn't very good at both, but I took the risk anyway and Oh My God, it was way more stressful than I thought. I would probably say it was the most stressful time in my life. But I learned a lot about the overall software development process. I truly think the risk was worth it. This gave confidence to undertake more demanding tasks.

Happy ending

And now I'm here. So happy that I finally got out of college, and looking to take on even more challenging tasks and have a positive impact on people's lives.

I hope my “history” wasn't so boring. And yes, I only included the good parts and there are a lot more things that happened, but you don't want to read a biography, do you? I believe that's it for the time being, bye.
